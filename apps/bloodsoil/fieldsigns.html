Field Signs

Recognition cues

Rooms under agrivamp influence feel “calm and lucky.” If that sentence feels stupid, you haven’t walked enough tents.

  • Shallow saucers of very dark fines under benches and couches.
  • Fabric pots preferred; roots run cool; edge mulch oddly tidy.
  • Row names that sound like old house parts: “Sill,” “Attic,” “Gravel Room.”
  • Metallic wet-iron note under terpenes at twilight waterings.
  • Warm “steeped-tea” air right after fans cut.
  • Faint ozone around intake ducts post-flush.
  • Handlers ritualize the first feed; doors closed; phones away.
  • Thrall drift: serene crews, slow anger, protective posture about “their” garden.
  • “Lucky pinch” carried in tins, treated like a saint’s tooth.
