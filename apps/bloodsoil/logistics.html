Turning Beds Into Coffins

Agrivampirism Supply Lines
The industry words are inputs and amendments. Their words are tether and cover. The same pallets that move calcium nitrate and coco coir also move pinches of homeland soil in sealed sachets, wake pads pre-salted, and drums of “tea” with no paperwork. The result is a sleeper who never feels far from the old house.
  • House → Pinch: soil scraped from inner sill or root cellar; dried in linen, vacuum-sealed.
  • Sire Drop: collected with silver lancet; one drop per drum, logged in a private ledger.
  • Broker: fronts as nutrient consultant; rides with shipments as “calibration tech.”
  • Grow Site: beds labeled by constellation, not row number; the sleeper claims a star.
  • Tea drums marked for “twilight application” and a fan-off window on the SOP board.
  • “Wake Pad” orders timed to harvest cycles; pads disposed in salted bins instead of compost.
  • Benches with old house nicknames: “Attic,” “Sill,” “Gravel Room.” Nobody on staff ever lived there.
  • A broker who always knows who you date, where you sleep, and when you changed your locks.
  • Track sachets; swap with sterilized fill; document the chain for court.
  • Quietly require boil for any “tea” at your jurisdiction’s facilities; watch sleepers wobble.
  • Redirect pad disposal to municipal incineration; confiscate residue for chemical signature.
The plants aren’t the target. The chemistry is. Hit the ritual and the vampire has to travel like the rest of us.
